Archimedes Capital

As a personal design project, Archimedes Capital was originally created as a way to learn and grow my front-end development skills. While that was a great learning experience, I felt the design was heavily influenced by only what I knew I was capable of developing with my front-end skills at the time. I decided to revisit the design of Archimedes to see if it could be pushed further into a more contemporary web design.

Original Approach

When concepting ideas for a new project I wanted to create a website design for an established industry, I settled on creating a fictional wealth management firm. I researched, designed, and coded a multipage website for this idea, with the goal of leveraging the Bootstrap front-end framework. I customized the design for some of the base Bootstrap components such as modals, buttons, forms, and navbars to create an experience that felt specialized for this idea. I developed and launched a completed website to showcase my idea and lives on today.

Setting Redesign Goals

I had always felt the Archimedes projectâ€™s design and branding played second to the coding challenges of learning Bootstrap. Given this, I decided to revisit the concept to do some exploration on where else I could take it. I placed some goals on the redesign to give myself a distinct idea of the intended outcome:

1.) The first was to enhance the overall usability of the website for an individual to navigate and find important information quickly and efficiently.

2.) The second was to develop a better sense of brand for Archimedes Capital. I wanted the site to resonate refined yet approachable, and for people to perceive them as trustworthy.

Reflection & Takeaways

After ideation and multiple rounds of concepting, I feel that the Archimedes Capital redesign has successfully met its goals. The final result is minimal and yet reflective of the subject matter that is financial services.

Visually, this design uses subtle structures to differentiate content sections, while allowing them to organically flow from one to the next. This results in a cohesive feel that provides an elevated visual experience over the generic two or three-column layout.

I would be cautious to consider the redesign a complete success until some proper usability testing occurs. Understanding how intuitive your website is will reduce the risk of building out features that may not meet your userâ€™s expectations.
