Archimedes Capital

Archimedes Capital is a personal design project that was originally created as a way to learn and grow my front-end coding skills. While a great learning experience, I felt the design was heavily influenced by only what I knew I was capable of coding at the time. With some free time I decided to revisit the concept and see how it could be pushed further.

Simple Beginnings

For a learning project I decided to design and code a website of all my own ideas. I went about creating a fictional wealth management firm I would call Archimedes Capital. With this new idea in mind, I did a little competitive analysis on existing firms to find the best approach to creating within such an established industry. I borrowed a little text from here and there to bring a realistic tone to the concept and worked up some simple services for the company to offer.

For the coding aspect of the project I utilized Bootstrap front-end frameworks to gather some new knowledge on how to work within an existing component library. I took the components and customized them to create an experience that felt specialized for this concept.

The design still lives on with a completed website of the original ideas.

Initializing a Redesign

I always felt the Archimedes project’s design and branding played second to the coding challenges of learning how to code with Bootstrap. Given this, I decided to revisit the concept to do some exploration on where else I could take it. I placed some simple goals on the redesign to give myself a distinct idea of the intended outcome:

1.) The first was to bring a contemporary design to the concept. With this enhancement would come improvements to the overall usability of the website. I wanted an individual to navigate and find important information quickly and efficiently.

2.) The second was to develop a better sense of the brand for Archimedes Capital. I wanted the site to feel refined yet approachable, and for people to perceive them as trustworthy. Choose colors to fit the attributes I had in mind when creating this firm.

Reflection & Takeaways

After some time with ideation and multiple rounds of concepting within Sketch, I feel that the Archimedes Capital redesign has successfully met the goals I set. The final result of the new design is minimal and yet reflective of the subject matter that is a refined, trustworthy financial firm.

Visually, this design uses subtle structures to differentiate content sections, while allowing them to organically flow from one element to the next. This results in a cohesive feel provides an elevated visual experience over the generic two or three-column layout.

I would be cautious to consider the redesign a complete success until some proper usability testing occurs. Understanding how intuitive your website is will reduce the risk of building out features that may not meet your user’s expectations. Design should serve purpose and not just style.
