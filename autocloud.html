AutoCloud

AutoCloud is a project that was designed to be a simple landing page for a SaaS business. I wanted to develop a concept that could help translate technical jargon into something more approachable and friendly for a novice user. I set out to achieve this goal by utilizing a balance of bright and muted colors coupled with illustrations for a more friendly approach to abstract ideas.

Initial Wireframing

I mocked up the initial layout, rough content structure, and overall flow using Balsamiq. This rough wireframing step was important to communicate ideas and content without visuals, to ensure a solid foundation before moving into visual design. For AutoCloud, particular attention was paid to documenting the intent of the features for the high fidelity phase of the design.

Iconography

Helpful Illustrations

Scalable vector graphics and line illustrations were chosen for this project as they are often associated with contemporary visual styles used in startups and technology-first companies. I also feel that the right illustrations can help convey complex technical ideas in a friendly manner while creating a memorable brand impression.

The beautiful illustrations I chose were sourced from the wonderfully talented Katerina Limpitsouni from unDraw illustrations. They were restyled slightly to match the brand colors.

Creating High Fidelity Designs

Since AutoCloud was a personal project, there was no existing brand to leverage for the visual design. Selecting brand attributes, color palette, typography, and illustration styles that were realistic enough to be plausible was an enjoyable challenge for me to undertake. The copy was sourced from actual technical websites to help give the concept a more authentic feel in place of lorem ipsum. All of the components were then brought together into Sketch to create final high fidelity designs.

Reflection & Takeaways

The AutoCloud concept emerged from the challenge of trying to discover the best method to reduce complex technical content into a simple user-friendly interface. Overall I am delighted with the result and feel the project accomplished the intent, though there are many possibilities for additional exploration.

I would enjoy the chance to build out additional pages like the product page or customer stories. I feel it would be an interesting design challenge to communicate customer problems and solutions effectively and simply. Additionally, I would explore and add interactions to this project like hovers and display animations.

If this was a project that would be shared with other developers to build then it would be shared through Figma or into a tool like Zeplin for the handoff between design and development.
