AutoCloud

The goal of this project was to design a simple landing page for a SaaS business. The intention was to create an experience that is vibrant and modern while keeping a minimal design. I wanted to design a website concept that could help translate technical jargon into something more approachable and friendly. This was to be achieved by using a balance of bright and muted colors and asymmetrical backgrounds to the illustrations with hopes of being more inviting.

Starting Out

During the wireframe phase I mocked up the initial layout, rough content structure, and overall flow using Balsamiq. This step was important to communicate ideas and content without visuals, to ensure a solid foundation before moving into visual design. For AutoCloud, particular attention was paid to documenting the intent of features for the high fidelity phase.

Iconography

Helpful Illustrations

Scalable vector graphics and line illustrations were chosen for this project as they are often associated with contemporary visual styles used in startups and technology-first companies. I also feel that the right illustrations can help convey complex technical ideas in a friendly manner while creating a memorable brand impression.

The beautiful illustrations I chose were sourced from the wonderfully talented Katerina Limpitsouni on unDraw illustrations. They were restyled to match the brand attributes and colors.

Creating High Fidelity Designs

Since AutoCloud was a personal project, there was not an existing brand to leverage for the visual design. Creating a brand that is realistic enough to be plausible was an enjoyable challenge for me to undertake. Selecting brand attributes, color palette, typography, and illustration styles that all work together is a project all by itself. The copy was sourced from actual technical websites to help give the concept a more authentic feel in place of lorem ipsum. All of the components were then brought together into Sketch to create high fidelity designs.

Reflection & Takeaways

The AutoCloud concept emerged from the challenge of trying to discover the best method to reduce complex technical content into a simple user-friendly interface. Overall I am delighted with the result and feel the project accomplished the intent, though there are many possibilities for additional exploration.

I would enjoy the chance to build out additional pages like the product page or customer stories. I feel it would be an interesting design challenge to communicate customer problems and solutions effectively and simply. Additionally, I would explore and add interactions to this project like hovers and display animations.

If this was a project that would be shared with other developers to build then the ensuing steps would be to put the designs into a tool like Zeplin for the handoff between design and development.
